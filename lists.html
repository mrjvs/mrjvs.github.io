The hype list

I'm not hyped about a lot of things. but when I am. I am REALLY hyped. So I compiled this list so that I don't forget about these when they finally release.

Links awakening

New installments in the Legend of Zelda franchise always make me super happy. This time it's a highly stylised version of an older |elda game. From the trailers, it seems to have a dungeon level editor included. It's very exciting; I trust it's going to be great fun.

Celeste chapter 9

Celeste is a great game with an amazing amount of detail and character. It's brutally hard but never punishing. It's a lot of content for its price but people wanted more. Celeste chapter 7 has over 100 new levels, new music and great level design. It releases quite soon as well.

The Stanley parable: Ultra Deluxe

The Stanley parable was an indie game that hit back in 2014. The narration and great humor is what made it so good. The funny way of story telling was good enough to come back to it every year even though I've memorized the entire script by now. The Ultra Deluxe version has more story and more endings. Releases on consoles.

Ori and the will of the wisps

The new Ori game has upgraded visuals, music and gameplay. It's everything I could of hoped for and more. The latest teaser trailer showed a whopping 5 boss battles and amazing new abilities for Ori. This is gonna be an amazing game for sure!

Hollow knight

The old ruined kingdom of hallownest has tons of things to explore and do. Many paths to explore, many bosses to fight and abilities to find. The game is hard and I love it to bits. Easily the best metroidvania I've played.

Celeste

Celeste is a game about mental illnesses and how to deal with them. You suspect just a platformer but what you get is the best level design you've ever seen and a story that has you engaged until the very last moment. I even went as far to do all of the extra hard levels.

Undertale

The illusion of choice is present in many games but Undertale isnt one of them. You have a choice and it actually matters. You can kill everyone or you can save everyone. Every choice has some consequence somewhere. (Plus its really fun with great music)

The legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild

I always thought that open world games are boring because much of it seems copy pasted but The legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild did it right for me. It embraced the nature of silence but there is tons of charming characters to be found if you just look around. You feel like you're a part of the world. (The soundtrack is a huge plus as well)
